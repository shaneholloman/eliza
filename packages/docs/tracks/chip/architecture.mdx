The E1 SoC is documented under `upstreams/research/chip/docs/arch/`. This page is the entry point.

## Top-level

- **Application processor (AP)** — development RISC-V AP at this stage; final design slots in a production-grade core. The pipeline, ISA extensions, and supervisor support are pinned in `docs/arch/`.
- **NPU** — purpose-built neural accelerator with contracts pinned in `compiler/runtime/` and verified by `verify/`.
- **DMA, interconnect, interrupts, memory** — independent RTL blocks in `rtl/` with cocotb tests in `verify/`.
- **Display** — block in `rtl/display/`; phone-class display path.
- **Boot ROM** — `fw/boot_rom/` plus an OpenSBI payload path under `fw/opensbi/`.

## NPU contract

The NPU is the part that matters for AI workloads. Its contract — what the compiler emits, what the runtime expects, what the verification harness checks — lives in `compiler/runtime/` (Python) and is shared between simulator, FPGA, and silicon paths. Any change to the NPU instruction stream must update the contract and re-verify.

## Memory hierarchy

The hierarchy and access patterns are pinned in `docs/arch/memory.md` (and adjacent files). DRAM modeling uses DRAMsim3 (`dramsim3.json`, `dramsim3.txt`).
